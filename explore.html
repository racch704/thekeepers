Biking

Cairngorm National Park is one of the best places in the world to mountain bike. The location is a mountain biker's heaven, with excellent natural routes, plenty of high technical climbs, and fast descents through breathtaking landscape. Walking

Whether you're an expert mountaineer or a weekend wanderer, the Cairngorm National Park has something for everyone. Choose from forested walks, muroes, moors, mountains, glens, or a potter through local villages. You don't have to drive for walks, as they are many just on our doorstep. Speak to Maria and Robert about walks in their region with spectacular views.

Water Sports

The two local water sports centres are Loch Insh and Loch Moirlich. These offer a range of activities including sailing, windsurfing, canoeing, kayaking and much more. The beautiful locations of both lochs provide the most incredible scenery for your time in the water.

Attractions

The Cairngorms National Park is unique in that, in addition to being full of open regions, it also has a variety of incredible attractions to see, many of which are award-winning. You can visit a museum or a castle, sample a dram on a distillery tour, pet free-roaming wild animals, or ride a rollercoaster. In the Cairngorms, you can shift from adrenaline junkie to art enthusiast.

Cairngorm attractions

Shopping

Small independent businesses, as well as some of the biggest names in outdoor apparel and gear, may be found all throughout Cairngorms National Park's towns and villages, offering quality Scottish (and frequently local) goods. Whether you're seeking for a one-of-a-kind souvenir, fine fresh Scottish produce, a fine local malt, local crafts, or equipment, you'll find it all here.
