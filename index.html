The Story of Glentruim Lodge
B&B and Pod


Comfortable Accommodation in the Heart of The Cairngorms National Park


Glentruim Lodge is situated between Newtonmore and Laggan, down a single track road a mile or so from the A9. It is situated within the Cairngorms National Park, and it’s a short walk to Glentruim Castle. Glentruim Lodge is settled in a blissfully peaceful location, with a duck pond and farmland to the front, and a mountain rising up magnificently at the back. It’s also at the heart of some of the Highland’s beautiful and majestic scenery, and close to a variety of outdoor activities. You can enjoy the local area for walking, mountain biking and running – straight from the doorstep. Yet you’re only a twenty minute drive to Aviemore, and all the outdoor pursuits, cafes and shops on offer there. Glentruim Lodge was once The Keepers, The Kennels next door has been converted into a whitewashed cottage. Over the last twenty years the Lodge has been transformed into a comfortable and welcoming family home, accommodating the growing needs of our family. As our older children have grown and flown the nest, we have chosen to keep that warm vibe by opening up the house to visitors as a homestay B&B with a relaxed family atmosphere.

Room bookings include continental breakfast. This consists of freshly baked bread, fruit juice, a selection of cereals, yoghurts, tea and freshly ground coffee. If you’re vegan, please let us know and we’ll be happy to provide suitable alternatives

Your Stay With us


What to Expect


It’s important to us that your stay here meets (hopefully exceeds!) your expectations. The Lodge is home to us, Maria and Robert, and to our two youngest children, as well as Furgus the dog. We share 3 of our bedrooms with our guests, sleeping up to 6 visitors at a time. We’ve been busy during lockdown accommodating key workers, but are happy to share our home with individuals, couples or family groups. You can also enjoy relaxing in our shared large, bright summer room, with us or our other guests. We also encourage you to cook in our kitchen if you’d like to prepare food, and provide space in the fridge and the kitchen cupboards so you can rustle something up. Robert bakes homemade bread, freshly brewed tea and coffee and we offer a wide selection of cereals and conserves for breakfast around the large kitchen table. There’s a plentiful supply of juices, and we always have dairy milk and plant based mylk. While we will be getting our two young people ready for school during term time, you can relax and help yourselves at your leisure. If you have a special diet and would like us to get something in for you, let us know when you book. We’re more than happy to help make your trip as comfortable and as carefree as possible.
